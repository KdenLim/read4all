What is Read4All?

A digital book club for curious students.

Read4All is a social-impact campaign that promotes reading habits, literacy awareness, and lifelong learning. It tackles low reading engagement caused by digital distractions and fragmented resources by bringing everything a reader needs into one warm, easy-to-use platform.

1

The Website

The central hub for campaign info, a resource finder for legal reading materials, book recommendations, weekly activities, and the pathway to join the community.

2

WhatsApp Community

A friendly group for book discussions, reflections, announcements, and peer support. Join it with one tap from any page of the website.

3

Instagram Campaign

@read4all__ shares book recommendations, quotes, quizzes, polls, mini-challenges, and storytelling reels that make literacy fun and visible.

Home

The interactive home page

  • Resources, Explore, Activities, Social Media and Guidelines bubbles jump straight to each section.

  • The top navigation bar stays available on every page.

Resources

Resource Finder: search legal reading materials

The heart of the site: a smart search shelf of legal, free, and authorized reading resources such as open libraries, public-domain ebooks, Google Books, and library catalogues.

  • Type a topic, e.g. classics, science, ESL, coding, Japanese, textbooks.
  • Filter by Best for (Children, Students, STEM, Research…) and Level (Kids, Teens, Adults, Academic).
  • Every result links to a legitimate source. No pirated PDFs, ever.

SDG 4

Our mission: Quality Education

Read4All directly supports SDG 4: Quality Education (target 4.6): inclusive, equitable education and lifelong learning for everyone. This page explains how small reading actions add up.

  • Legal reads: fair access to free reading materials
  • Book picks: making the next read less overwhelming
  • Peer support: a warm space to learn together
